About Us

We believe ideas matter. Good ideas can change the world for the better. People often don’t get the training they need to share those ideas with the widest possible audience in ways that matter. We’re here to help.

Polly Thistlethwaite is an academic librarian, activist and innovator. In the pre-internet 1980s, she joined the activist group ACTUP and helped activists and PWAs gain access to libraries, the only place where life-saving medical information was available. Since then, medical journals and most scholarly work has been digitized, but it is still mostly locked behind paywalls. She is devoted to changing the system of scholarly communication so that more academic work is available to more readers. You can find her on Twitter as @MissReadings.

Jessie Daniels is an author, scholar-activist and innovator. Daniels is author or editor of five academic books and dozens of scholarly journal articles. Her bylines have appeared in the New York Times, Newsweek, Entropy Magazine, and is a regular columnist at Huffington Post. She has a wide range of experience as a university professor, senior producer at an Internet company, and as researcher at Rikers Island Adolescent Unit. She is devoted to shifting paradigms toward justice. You can find her on Twitter as @JessieNYC.

In Our Webinar Series

You'll Learn

How to Set up a Digital Platform

There are so many ways to be online, it can be confusing to know how to get started. We will help you establish a digital platform that makes sense for you.

How to Manage Your Time

One of the biggest concerns scholars have about making their work widely available is that it takes too much time. We will help you establish time-saving work flows.

How to Make Your Work Count

Whether you work in a traditional university setting, a non-profit or a grassroots organization, you want your ideas to change the world. We will show you how to begin.

How to Protect Yourself

These are challenging times, when public scholars and other thought leaders are often attacked online in ways that have real consequences offline. We will show you how to protect yourself from attacks, and what to do if you become a target.

We offer coaching, editing, and help with negotiating book contracts.

Work with Us

We were inspired to begin this by Kerry Ann Rockquemore and Jessica Ayo Alabi, both scholars, innovators and women of color. To pay forward their inspiration, we want to work with you if you have a WOC or QPOC run business that’s adjacent to ours, get in touch. We’d love to hear from you.
